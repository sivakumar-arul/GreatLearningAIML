Statistical Learning Project2¶

Attribute Information:

age : age of primary beneficiary

sex : insurance contractor gender, female, male

bmi : Body mass index, providing an understanding of body,weights that are relatively high or low relative to height, objective index of body weight (kg / m ^ 2) using the ratio of height to weight, ideally 18.5 to 24.9

children : Number of children covered by health insurance /Number of dependents

smoker : Smoking

region : the beneficiary's residential area in the US, northeast,southeast, southwest, northwest.

charges : Individual medical costs billed by health insurance.

  # calculate interquartile range
q25, q75 = np.percentile(ins['bmi'], 25), np.percentile(ins['bmi'], 75)
iqr = q75 - q25
print('Percentiles: ')
print('25th=%.3f, 75th=%.3f, IQR=%.3f' % (q25, q75, iqr))
# calculate the outlier cutoff
cut_off = iqr * 1.5
lower, upper = q25 - cut_off, q75 + cut_off
# identify outliers
outliers = [x for x in ins['bmi'] if x < lower or x > upper]
print('Identified outliers for BMI : %d' % len(outliers))
# remove outliers
outliers_removed = [x for x in ins['bmi'] if x >= lower and x <= upper]
print('Non-outlier observations BMI : %d' % len(outliers_removed))

4a. Do charges of people who smoke differ significantly from the people who don't?

H0 : Charges of People who smoke = charges of People who don't smoke

H1 : Charges of People differ significantly between smoke and non smoking

H0 : smoke_grp['charges'].mean() = nosmoke_grp['charges'].mean()

H1 : smoke_grp['charges'].mean() != nosmoke_grp['charges'].mean()

alpha = 0.05
